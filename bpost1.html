Developing a reading habit

  • Francis Muliyunda
  • April 1, 2022

The very first lesson we pick up from formal education is that of reading and writing. Without these skills, it is very hard to learn anything else in formal education. We carry these skills throughout our education and they help us navigate the world with ease. But despite this reading background, our generation has little interest in reading books. Instead, we are preoccupied with social media that we never ever contemplate reading books. Past generations had almost ritualistic reading habits. My grandmother always tells me how newspapers were an essential part of their reading culture (indirectly telling me how our generation does not have interest in reading).

The reading culture in our generation is poor for various reasons. One of the biggest contributors is the widespread use of social media which people use for most parts of their daily lives. Social media has short entertainment content which people find really easy to consume rather than reading, say, a news article with over five paragraphs. I will try to help you realize that reading is one of the best habits you can develop in order to help you navigate the 21st century better.

Read a story each day to always learn

BENEFITS OF READING

  1. Mental Stimulation

    2. Different body parts have to undergo different types of exercises to be in good shape and function optimally. The brain is no different. Reading is one of the best exercises you can give your brain. The mental stimulation helps the brain retain its power and capacity as well as slows down the effects of stress for the reader.

  2. Memory Improvement

    3. We heavily rely on our memory for everything we do than we actually realize. Even for something as basic as directions to a place we go to everyday (or in serious situations like a job interview when trying to remember the core values of a company that you were reading the night prior) is entirely dependent on memory. A natural desire to improve our memory should develop when we realize how important our memory is for us to function. When you read books, you have to memorize names and places and other aspects to comprehend the story of the book. This in turn helps improve your memory way more than other prescibed methods of memory improvement.

  3. Vocabulary Improvement

    4. Developing a reading habit is one of the best ways to develop your vocabulary. We all find ourselves in situations where we need to address people. It can be that you tasked with writing a eulogy or having to communicate a message to your co-workers in writing. For these situations, you need to have a good vocabulary to help you sound smart and clear in expressing yourself. This is where reading comes in. Exposing yourself to different genres of books helps in improving your vocabulary.

  4. Attention Span Increase

    5. Our (ab)use of social media has greatly affected our reading culture and subsequently our attention span. Social media is comprised of short messages (mostly about entertainment) and this has made people to be lazy at reading longer pieces. Our brains have gotten used to reading short content that being confronted with serious information publications stresses the brain. Our attention span has greatly decclined over the years according to a number of studies on the negative impact of social media. However, reading has been known to help increase our attention spans if one develops it as a habit. Improving your attention span helps you focus on important details in different situations. When you have a reading habit, studying at school becomes easy. You will assimilate knowledge and become a better learner than someone who 'studies' for the sake of passing a test or an exam.

  5. Develops Knowledge 'Thirst'

    6. Be it lunchtime debates at work, awkward chats with fellow men at the funeral firewood at midnight or when trying to impress someone you're attracted to while waiting for the lecturer to come, we all want to come out very articulate and show how informed we are about a given topic. Once you develop interest in reading books, you will find yourself drifting through different information and acquiring knowledge on different topics and coming out smartly opinionated.

Book Review Corner

This website aims at helping our generation develop reading habits and hopefully improve reading culture. Thus, I introduce to you the 'Book Review Corner'. This is a section where we share a book and set a time-frame for the book to be read and reviewed at the end of that period. You are at liberty to share suggestions for books to be read as well as share insight on your thoughts about the book under review.

Any specific books?

We encourage reading and reviewing books written by Zambians and Africans at large. There are no restictions with book genres, be it history, love, entrpreneurship, philosophy and so on. The goal of reading and reviewing different genres is to help you discover your taste in books as well as acquire knowledge through reading.

Feel free to write to us about your suggestions and direction of the Book Review Corner.
